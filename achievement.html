OUR ACHIEVEMENTS

Inventions/Discoveries

  • First and foremost (ha), Harvard is the oldest institution of higher education in the United States, founded (under the name "New College") in 1636 by vote of the Great and General Court of the Massachusetts Bay Colony.
  • In a similar vein, the Harvard Corporation is the United States' first chartered corporation.
  • The Harvard Crimson is the nation's oldest continually published college newspaper, and The Harvard Advocate is the nation's oldest continually published college literary magazine.
  • The first continuous chapter of Phi Beta Kappa was founded at Harvard in 1871.

Athletics

  • Harvard Stadium, built in 1903, was the first collegiate athletic stadium in the United States. It also pioneered the use of reinforced concrete in construction.
  • The first intercollegiate athletic competition was a rowing competition between Harvard and Yale on Lake Winnipesaukee in New Hampshire held on August 3, 1852. This race also made Harvard the first victors of an intercollegiate athletic competition :)
  • The first baseball catcher's mask was used in a Harvard game in 1877.
  • Harvard Law student William H. Lewis became the first black American to play college football in 1892.

VOICES OF THE BEST

US President Barack Obama entered Harvard Law School in 1988 and eventually became the first black president of the Harvard Law Review. During his time at the school, he also played basketball on the black law students' association team.

Barack Obama

Former US vice-president Al Gore graduated cum laude from Harvard in 1969 with a degree in government. He wrote a great senior thesis titled, "The Impact of Television on the Conduct of the Presidency, 1947-1969."

Al Gore

Bill Gates began studying at Harvard in 1973 and decided to major in applied math. He dropped out two years later to found Microsoft with his friend Paul Allen. One of Gates' former roommates told Harvard Magazine that Gates would study for 36 hours at a time, with 10 hour breaks in between.

Bill Gates
